The University

The National University of Colombia is a public and national research university in Colombia, with general campuses in Bogotá, Medellín, Manizales and Palmira. It was established in 1867 by an act of the Congress of Colombia. The University is part of what is known as the Golden Triangle of higher education in Colombia, being among the most selective and competitive universities in the country.

The Faculty

The Faculty of Mines is an institution that is mainly taught in engineering education in twelve of its branches, standing out for being the faculty with the highest amount of undergraduate engineering programs in Colombia, in addition to being considered the most prestigious in the country.
